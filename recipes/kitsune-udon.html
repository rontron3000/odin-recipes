Kitsune Udon

Description

Kitsune Udon is a Japanese noodle soup in dashi broth, topped with seasoned fried tofu, pink-swirl narutomaki fish cake, and scallions. This hearty udon soup is one of the most popular, classic Japanese noodle dishes.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 cups of dashi broth
  • 1 Tbsp of mirin
  • 1 tsp of sugar
  • 1 Tbsp of usukuchi soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp of kosher salt
  • 2 servings of udon
  • 4 Inari age
  • 1 green onion/scallion
  • 4 slices of narutomaki
  • schichimi togarashi (optional)

Steps

  1. In a saucepan, add the dashi, mirin, sugar, soy sauce, and salt and bring it to a boil. Once boiling, turn off the heat or cover and keep on a low simmer.
  2. Squeeze the excess liquid from the Inari age (or you can keep it as is). Cut the green onion into thin slices. Cut the narutomaki fish cake into â…›-inch (3 mm) slices.
  3. Bring a large pot of water to a boil for the udon noodles. My favorite udon is frozen Sanuki udon. Reheat the frozen udon noodles in boiling water for 1 minute (no need to defrost). If you use dry noodles, follow the package instructions.
  4. Pick up the noodles in a strainer or drain the hot water. Make sure to shake off the excess water (which will end up diluting your soup).
  5. Divide the drained udon noodles into individual serving bowls. Pour the hot broth over the noodles to cover. Top with the Inari age, narutomaki, and green onions. Sprinkle the shichimi togarashi on top (optional).
