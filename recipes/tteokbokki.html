Tteokbokki Recipe

Description

Tteokbokki is a Korean side dish that is made of Korean rice cakes, cooked in a spicy sauce, and garnished with sesame seeds and green onions.

Ingredients

  • 1lb of cylinde-shaped rice cake
  • 4 cups of water
  • 7 large size dried anchovies, with heads and intestines removed
  • 6"x8" dried kelp
  • 1/3 cup of gochujang (Korean hot pepper paste)
  • 1 Tbsp of gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 1 Tbsp of sugar
  • 3 green onions, cut into 3" long pieces
  • 2 hardboiled eggs
  • 1/2lb of fish cakes (optional)

Steps

  1. Add the water, dried anchovies, and dried kelp to a shallow pot or pan.
  2. Boil for 15 minutes over medium high heat without the lid.
  3. Combine hot pepper paste, hot pepper flakes, and sugar in a small bowl. Remove the anchovies and kelp from the pot and add the rice cake, the mixture in the bowl, the green onion, and the optional fish cakes and hard boiled eggs. The stock will be about 2Â½ cups.
  4. Stir gently with a wooden spoon when it starts to boil. Keep stirring until the rice cake turns soft and the sauce thickens and looks shiny, which should take about 10 -15 minutes. If the rice cake is not soft enough, add more water and continue stirring until soften. When you use freshly made rice cake, it takes shorter time. If you use frozen rice cake, thaw it out and soak in cold water to soften it before cooking.
  5. Remove from the heat and serve hot.
